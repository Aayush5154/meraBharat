Kabaddi - The Ancient Sport

About Kabaddi

Kabaddi is an ancient Indian sport that dates back thousands of years. It is a team sport played between two teams, with each team taking turns to send a "raider" into the opponent's half to tag as many defenders as possible and return safely to their own half.

Rules and Gameplay

The game is played on a rectangular court, and the raider must tag as many opponents as possible while chanting "kabaddi, kabaddi." Defenders, on the other hand, try to stop the raider and prevent them from returning to their half. Kabaddi has various formats, including Standard style, Circle style, and Beach Kabaddi.

Historical Significance

Kabaddi has deep cultural and historical roots in India. It was played in ancient times as a means of physical fitness and self-defense. Today, Kabaddi has gained international recognition, with leagues and tournaments held around the world.

Kabaddi Worldwide

While Kabaddi originated in India, it has become a global sport with a growing fan base. Countries like Iran, South Korea, and Bangladesh have excelled in international Kabaddi competitions. Leagues such as the Pro Kabaddi League (PKL) in India have contributed to the sport's popularity.
