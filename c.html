Taj Mahal - A Symbol of Love

About the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a stunning white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Construction started in 1632 and was completed in 1653.

Architecture

The Taj Mahal is renowned for its intricate and symmetrical architecture. The main building features a large dome and four minarets, with a reflection pool in front. The white marble facade is adorned with delicate inlay work of precious and semi-precious stones.

Historical Significance

As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Taj Mahal is a symbol of eternal love and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. It attracts millions of visitors from around the world who marvel at its beauty and historical significance.

Visit the Taj Mahal

If you plan to visit the Taj Mahal, make sure to check the official website for the latest information on tickets, visiting hours, and any COVID-19 related guidelines. A trip to the Taj Mahal is a memorable experience, allowing you to witness one of the wonders of the world.
